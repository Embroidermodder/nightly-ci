Undo History
Also referred to as just 'History', it is a dockable widget and it resides in either the left or right dockable widget areas, next to the toolbars. It keeps track of every object that is added or deleted. It keeps track of modifications to the objects, such as moving, rotating and scaling objects. It also keeps track of navigating the view by panning (with the middle mouse button) or zooming (with the mouse wheel). When keeping track of the navigation, it only keeps track of the initial and final destinations. If you zoom in/out or pan multiple times in this process, only one entry for navigation will appear in the history. This eliminates the tedious undo process that plagues some applications.
Showing and Hiding the History
To show or hide the history, you can right click in the menu bar area or in the toolbar areas and a context menu will appear. Check or uncheck the name 'History' listed in the context menu to show and hide it. The history can also be hidden by clicking the 'X' button in the top corner of the history widget.
Docking and Undocking the History
To undock the history, click on the title and drag it with your mouse. This will pop the widget out of its current position and make it into a floating window. You can position this window to your liking virtually anywhere on the screen. To redock the widget, just drag and drop it with the titlebar to the right or left dockable widget area and it will dock itself. The history can also be docked or undocked by clicking the 'diamond' button in the top corner of the history widget.

Undoing and Redoing
The history is populated with new entries as you add and manipulate objects in the view. The most recent entries appear below previous entries. You can move backwards and forwards in time by selecting individual entries in the history.

When undoing a particular action using the history, the entries are not cleared until you perform another action. By clicking and dragging along the entries, you will visually see the view update to that particular point in time.

Typing 'U', 'UNDO' or 'REDO' into the command prompt, will also step forward or backwards through the undo history.
You may also use the system standard keyboard shortcut for undo (Ctrl+Z) and redo (Ctrl+Y or Ctrl+Shift+Z). The shortcuts for undo/redo may vary depending on your operating system.

There is no limit to how many actions you can undo, but you cannot go back any further than when the file was opened.
