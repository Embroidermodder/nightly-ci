  • 3D Foam
    This foam is applied to the material that will be stitched. The foam should be larger than the desired raised area and should closely match the thread color being used. It is then stitched over and becomes part of the final product. Remaining foam on the outside of the raised area is then removed and any exposed foam has to be carefully poked inside the stitching. This embroidery technique is commonly seen on embroidered baseball caps.

  • 3D Embroidery
    This refers to embroidery designs that have elevated or raised areas. This affect can be achieved by building up the underlying stitches, with 3D Foam, or by using 3D Applique or 3D Lace techniques.

  • Bean Stitch
    This type of stitch moves between two points three times. This puts the last stitch of the bean in the starting position of the next bean stitch. The name comes from the resulting appearance resembling a bean shape since the thread in between the two stitch points pops out slightly. To calculate the number of actual stitches (b) required to make a bean stitch, multiply the number of consecutive stitch points (n) by three and then add one:

    b = 3n+1

    For example, if stitching a square with corner points A-B-C-D, the order of stitching would be:

    A-B-A-B-C-B-C-D-C-D-A-D-A

P - Embroidery Terminology
  • Pantograph
    Hoops attach to the pantograph. It is responsible for movement of the X-Axis and Y-Axis of the embroidery machine. The pantograph moves inversely to the stitch coordinates in the embroidery design.
