Terminology
There are three main types of terminology that you will need to be familiar with, CAD, CAM and embroidery.

In CAD software, much of the terminology used comes from drafting, geometry, architecture and other design concepts. The features provided in CAD software can be quite varied, some being simple concepts, while others being more complex but much more powerful. CAD software is a type of vector graphics software, but typically differs by providing much higher precision editing and creation tools, and having a larger focus on the data that the objects represent.

In CAM software, much of the terminology used comes from manufacturing and machining. This is because the software actions that are taken to prepare files for a machine often mimic the actual processes used in creation of the final product. Many of the terms described here also describe how the machine operates and various parts that make the machine work.

In embroidery software, much of the terminology used is based on either traditional hand embroidery means and methods, naming conventions for specialized stitch types, and others originating from the early days of computerized machine embroidery.
